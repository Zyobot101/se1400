Website Summary:

What content does Rookie Coding feature?

As the name "Rookie Coding" implies, this is a space where you can sample my upcoming coding projects that I create in my spare time, mostly games, small exercises, and beginner-friendly challenges to start. Although I'm a beginning coder and don't have much (if any) experience on my belt, I see the chance to share my process with an audience and take your comments, suggestions, and (constructive) criticism as much-welcome practice and an opportunity to build a community where we can learn and grow in our coding together.

Having said that, don't expect too many advanced projects or market-ready products from me. I'm a hobbyist and a learner, not a professional developer with a team or a business behind me. For the time being, expect mostly "DIY" or "homebrew" things, as well as the SE-1400 course homepage with links to the usual course assignments and final-project milestones. After all, that's why this site's being built in the first place.

Where do I find content for Rookie Coding, namely text and images?

There's an ever-growing host of media on the World Wide Web already, so in theory, finding text, images, and other content should be easy. Unfortunately, IP laws and attribution courtesy — while absolutely necessary — limits my choices vastly.

Per the above, I will mostly stick to my own content in the form of my upcoming coding projects. Having said that, visual aids are always helpful, so I may incorporate certain images, videos, and excerpts from my favorite YouTubers and online creators when they demo a concept or project idea particularly well (especially if their sites have a "Share" button that encourages spreading their content elsewhere). That said, giving due credit to the original publisher remains essential, so a clear indication will be made on anything that's not originally mine. Otherwise, I will be mindful of Fair Use guidelines and prohibitions on plagiarism.

Why should you care about Rookie Coding?

While I understand the appeal of more "put-together" programming sites or looking to professional coders for guidance, Rookie Coding provides a more ground-level, relatable take on what it's like to be a beginner programmer learning the ins and outs, making mistakes, and developing his process for the first time. I may not get everything right immediately, but a site that showcases what I've come up with and what I still need to do is a way to teach beginner programmers by meeting them where they're at — and more importantly, redoubling that it's okay to still be figuring things out (provided you learn from your mistakes and can meet deadlines and such). If nothing else, Rookie Coding's a learning opportunity for me (and hopefully, also you), and I'm excited for us to learn and grow together.

Why build a whole Rookie Coding site?

Besides fulfilling requisites for the SE-1400 course, a website affords me both a platform for sharing my coding projects and creating a community for fellow beginners. Sure, I could upload my projects to an existing site or platform, but that wouldn't be as "self-directed" or encourage me to develop as strong a foundation as I document my learning journey and try to provide something that I think is lacking in most online spaces. On most sites, I'd be one user among many and might be easier to gloss over, but on my own site, it's easier for people to take note of what I'm up to and what I hope to share with them.

What would I like you to do here on Rookie Coding?

For starters, feel free to browse around and check out my coding projects. Again, please note that they'll mostly be games, small exercises, and beginner-friendly challenges to start out, so nothing professional or extraordinary to start out with. That said, while I don't have a comments section yet, your engagement and feedback would be valued, so don't be afraid to leave comments, suggestions, and other (constructive) criticism. I hope to build a bit of a coding beginners' community here, and I believe this is a good way to start.

What's the goal of Rookie Coding?

Primarily, I wanted a space where I could share my coding projects and receive others' feedback, and that hasn't changed. But ultimately, I also want to make programming accessible and a bit more user-friendly for beginners, and figured that a site that documents the learning journey of a beginner writing and trudging through the learning process for and alongside fellow beginners would make it seem more approachable, more learnable, and more relatable than a polished, put-together site or business might. The idea is to encourage understanding and curiosity, not to deter or intimidate newcomers right out the gate.
