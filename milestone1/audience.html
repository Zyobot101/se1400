The Target Audience:

Who am I making this for?

Although users from all walks of life are welcome, my main target audience are fellow college-aged students and other young adults who are interested in coding and programming, yet unsure where to start. To that end, I'd expect most users to range from 18 to 20-something, and mainly skew male on account of the usual gender balance in the software engineering and development fields (though anyone of any age, sex, gender, or other demographic is equally welcome).

Lastly, while I don't have high hopes about this site going viral or making it into the history books any time soon, I'd expect my audience to be widely dispersed (but mainly English-speaking, since this site is an English-language site). That said, I am American and write in American English, so coding projects, updates, and other content will cater towards an American audience by default. Nevertheless, coding and programming know no geographic, national, or cultural boundary, so as long as users possess English literacy and an interest in the relevant topics, feel free to stop by and hang out.

Why am I making this for you?

First off, I've been — and in fact, still am — in the shoes of someone who's always found coding, programming, and understanding how computers work to be intimidating and hard to wrap my head around, given all the jargon and the highly technical nature of the subject. And while I may be candid about it, I very much doubt I'm the only one who feels this way or has felt discouraged by the way the subject is presented, taught, and talked about by those "in the know" or endowed with a natural talent for it. Combined with my budding technical writing skillset, and I've decided the best way to teach both myself and make the subject a bit more approachable to others is to take the opportunity to write about it and post my coding projects (as amateur as they be).

Moreover, while there are plenty of coding boot camps and other more "polished" options, those can come across as intimidating, and maybe even as a deterrent to newcomers overwhelmed by all the new terminology, flashy graphics, and ease with which the creators and programmers — who overwhelmingly tend to be seasoned professionals or specialists in their line of work — toss around seamlessly and without difficulty. The rest of us, meanwhile, are struggling to follow and have to brute-force our way through exercises and develop a semi-makeshift way of picking it up ourselves. For some people, it clicks after a while. But for others, a clear path forward or how the actual learning process works is simply not laid out for them. With luck, I hope to help resolve that discrepancy.

What can you expect here?

As I've said before, Rookie Coding is a beginner-friendly space where I post my coding projects, so expect mostly games, small exercises, beginner-friendly challenges, and a few posts from me about how things are going. While I don't have the know-how to set up a comments section at this time, it is a venue for building community engagement and receiving your comments and feedback, so rest assured that I will look into that.

Do not, however, expect any professional or extraordinary projects or feats anytime soon. While I am a beginner and a hobbyist, I am no expert or career developer. I don't have a team or a business behind me, so what you see here will reflect the skill level of someone slowly, but surely developing their foundation and figuring things out as he goes along. Improvement and larger undertakings will come with time, but don't expect a quantum leap from Tic-Tac-Toe to my first physics engine overnight.
