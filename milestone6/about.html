Who am I?

First off, welcome to my site! My name is Ethan, and as you might figure, I'm the humble owner of Rookie Coding. As the name of my website implies, I'm just an aspiring beginner coder figuring things out one at a time and building up his skills piece by piece, despite a lack of natural talent and other stumbling blocks that come with a completely different set of lingo, practices, and workspaces than what I'm used to. A bit more context, if you'll permit me:

Raised in the Great State of Minnesota — 10,000+ lakes, Vikings fandom, and mountains of snow and metric tons of ice every winter — I attended the University of St. Thomas for my B.S. in Business Economics (with an Applied Statistics minor), graduating the May before my family started packing up moving halfway across the country to the (Equally) Great State of Utah — and speaking for myself, I haven't looked back since.

Having found Utah Tech University and started working on my Master's degree there, I also figured it'd be wise to take some Computer Science classes on the side and brush up on a skill I had neglected and left underdeveloped (despite the technical nature of my undergraduate degree and the modern world we live in). To be honest, I was never really a coding guru and was always intimidated by all the jargon and endless lines of code that made all my favorite apps and software work — but at the same time, the fact coders could build all kinds of cool stuff with Python in their pocket, C++ in their veins, or Java in their repertoire wowed me and convinced me it was worth studying (even if I never mastered it fully). If nothing else, it would also look good on my resume here in this high-tech, wired-up world we're now living in.

And now, here I am, taking my first Utah Tech Comp-Sci class and building this site as my first major project. It may not be eye-candy or the most prize-worthy software you've ever laid eyes on, but I'm excited for us to learn and start on our coding journeys together. So please, do stick around!

Why did I post this site?

As I said above, Rookie Coding was spurred by my first Utah Tech Comp-Sci class, which focuses on HTML5 and front-end web design. Naturally, this site is proof of concept and a real-world demonstration of the skills I've acquired in class, and while it's far from perfect or pristine, it lays a solid foundation that I will build on in future.

Besides fulfilling course requirements, this site also serves as an opportunity to document my learning journey and share it with others, allowing it to both be of help to fellow beginner coders with little clue where to start and a potential gateway to collaboration and community if there's enough interest. If nothing else, it also keeps me somewhat "accountable" on my learning journey and coding projects: despite my ambition and interest in all the cool stuff you can do with programming knowledge under your belt, I've long put off actually building up my skills and let my misgivings and intimidation get the better of me — and now, that's got to change.

Now, I finally have a platform to post my projects and experiments. They may not be as slick and professional as what professional programmers and software engineers can cook up with their eyes closed, but at least they're something — and for now, that's enough.

What can you expect here?

In short, expect a few amateur-level projects and my first experiments in coding something meaningful: puzzles, games, and maybe a very rudimentary app or two once I've hit my stride and am feeling ambitious enough to market or share my content elsewhere. As I am a beginner (and Rookie Coding is a beginner-oriented site), please don't expect expert-level content or anything market-ready — I'm not a professional engineer, I don't have a team behind me, and I may very well go the rest of my life having access to neither.

That said, I also have a "Blog" page, which is where you can find my blog posts giving English-language updates and rundowns of the projects, research, and other developments and miscellaneous thoughts that mark my coding journey or otherwise warrant posting here on Rookie Coding for everyone's edification. Games and exercises are fun and are the ultimate practical demonstration of applied, real-world programming skill, but breaking it down and talking shop in a language we all (presumably) understand — in this case, Good Ol' American English — lends context and clarification that a finished product or lines of code don't provide on their own.

For now, Rookie Coding's still new on the scene, so while it's just starting out and while I'm first branching out, expect a trickle of blog posts and maybe one or two actual coding projects on the "Projects" page. I intend to deliver and pick up steam as it gets easier, but again, I am a beginner who's just starting out and has little preexisting skill with coding, so your patience (and preferably, constructive feedback) would be much appreciated.
